Vidhana Soudha

The Vidhana Soudha in Karnataka is a magnificent granite building housing the state legislature and secretariat, showcasing Mysore-Neo Dravidian architecture with Indo-Saracenic and Dravidian influences.

Bengaluru Palace

Bengaluru Palace, a regal architectural masterpiece, is a popular tourist attraction in Karnataka. It features a massive campus, elegant interiors, and a well-maintained exterior with greenery, hosting cultural events and exhibitions.

Learn More

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden in Bengaluru is a 240-acre oasis featuring over 1,854 plant species, a historic glass house, a serene lake, and diverse wildlife. Established in 1760 by Hyder Ali and expanded by Tipu Sultan, it is a beloved green space and popular tourist attraction in the city.

About
Bengaluru

Bangalore, officially Bengaluru, has a rich history dating back to 1537. It is the capital of Karnataka, with a population of over 8 million and an area of 2196 sq. km. The city has a strong financial status, being a major hub for technology and software industries.

Temples

Bengaluru's historic temples include the Bull Temple (Nandi) with its colossal statue, Dodda Ganapathi Temple featuring a large Ganesha idol, and Gavi Gangadhareshwara Temple known for its unique Makar Sankranti solar phenomenon. The ISKCON Temple, a modern gem dedicated to Lord Krishna, and the ancient Someshwara Temple highlight the city's rich spiritual heritage.

Iskon Temple

The ISKCON Temple in Bangalore is a spiritual landmark renowned for its stunning architecture, serene atmosphere, and vibrant Krishna consciousness activities, attracting devotees and tourists alike from around the world.

Basaveswara Temple

The Basaveshwara Temple in Karnataka is dedicated to Lord Basaveshwara, a revered 12th-century philosopher and social reformer. It stands as a symbol of his teachings promoting equality, social justice, and devotion to Lord Shiva.

Learn More

Kote Venkataramana

Kote Venkataramana Temple in Bangalore, dedicated to Lord Venkataramana, is an ancient Hindu temple known for its Dravidian architecture and religious significance. It is a spiritual hub attracting devotees seeking blessings and solace in its sacred ambiance.

Famous Personnels

Discover Karnataka's notable figures: Siddaramaiah, the current CM; Punneth Rajkumar, a beloved actor; Yash Gowda, a rising star; and Rahul Dravid, cricket legend, each contributing uniquely to Karnataka's cultural and political landscape.

Siddaramaiah

Siddaramaiah is a veteran politician who has served as the Chief Minister of Karnataka twice, from 2013-2018 and now in 2024. He is a mass leader popular among all sections of society, particularly the backward classes.

Yash Gowda

Yash, also known as Naveen Kumar Gowda, is a popular Kannada actor who has achieved widespread recognition in India. He gained pan-India fame with the 2018 blockbuster "K.G.F: Chapter 1" and its sequel in 2022.

Puneeth Rajkumar

Puneeth Rajkumar was a beloved Kannada film star known for his versatility, charisma, and philanthropic work. He passed away in 2021 at age 46. Puneeth's biography will be included in the Kannada school syllabus.

Rahul dravid

Rahul Dravid, nicknamed "The Wall", is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in cricket history. He played 164 Tests and 344 ODIs for India between 1996-2012, scoring over 24,000 international runs.
